Digital Public Infrastructure and Taxes: Pakistan's High-Stakes Experiment

A.Z. Farooqui, F. Chatha, F. Santoro, F. Muhammad & W.A. Bajwa · June 19, 2025

Examines how Digital Public Infrastructure—digital identity, payment systems, and data exchange networks—can modernize Pakistan's tax administration. Discusses FBR's deployment of digital tools including NADRA integration, the FBR Maloomat nudging portal, Iris (e-filing), Track and Trace (production monitoring), Point of Sale systems, and digital invoicing through Hubble. Addresses obstacles including integration gaps between agencies and privacy concerns regarding third-party data sharing.

Pakistan's Support for Wealth Taxes

M. Gallien, U. Javed, V. van den Boogaard (with contributions from A.Z. Farooqui & F. Chatha) · June 24, 2025

Discusses findings from the first comprehensive nationwide survey of Pakistani attitudes toward wealth taxation, polling 7,500 adults in 2024. Challenges the assumption that the public resists progressive taxation: over 40% support a general wealth tax, and support increases substantially for specific proposals with thresholds. Higher-income individuals are actually more likely to support new wealth taxes. Trust in government emerges as a critical determinant.

Tax Reforms and the Battle for Pakistan's Future in the 2024 Elections

A.Z. Farooqui & F. Chatha · February 7, 2024

Analyzes the competing tax and fiscal policy visions of Pakistan's three major political parties ahead of the 2024 general elections. Identifies critical policy gaps in all party platforms: untaxed agricultural income (agriculture is one-quarter of GDP), underperforming property taxation, and the need for genuine local government empowerment. Pakistan's 10.3% tax-to-GDP ratio significantly underperforms the South Asian average of 19%.

Consulting · SROI / Value for Money

Social Return on Investment & Value-for-Money Evaluation of an FCDO Governance & Anti-GBV Programme

Lead SROI / VfM consultant · FCDO-funded programme managed by the British Council, Pakistan · 2026

A Social Return on Investment (SROI) and Value-for-Money (VfM) evaluation of a multi-district governance, social cohesion, and gender-based-violence prevention programme across Khyber Pakhtunkhwa and Punjab. The study monetised the social value of verified programme outcomes using locally grounded replacement-cost proxies, traced wider economic effects through input–output multipliers, and valued efficiency gains to government systems—benchmarked against the FCDO Value-for-Money (5E) framework. Methods: difference-in-differences impact estimation, replacement-cost monetisation, SAM/input–output multipliers, and time-driven activity-based costing of public services.

Consulting · Education-Sector Analysis

Secondary Analysis of Punjab MICS 2024: Education Sector

With Dr. Faisal Bari · for the Punjab Economic Research Institute (PERI), Planning & Development Board · 2026

A district-level secondary analysis of the Punjab Multiple Indicator Cluster Survey (MICS) 2024 for the provincial government, diagnosing foundational learning, early-childhood participation, and equity gaps in access and outcomes, and linking them to development spending and service-delivery inputs across 36 districts. The report organises the evidence around a child life-cycle framework to inform the provincial education budget and medium-term sector plan. Methods: equity and parity diagnostics, district benchmarking, and spending–outcome analysis.

The Asia Foundation

COVID-19 & The New Normal for Women in the Economy: Pakistan

Faisal Bari & Ahsan Zia Farooqui · The Asia Foundation & IDEAS · 2020

A comprehensive study exploring the multidimensional impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic on women in Pakistan's informal economy. The research surveyed workers across sub-sectors with the highest female informal employment: home-based workers, domestic services, salons and beauticians, agriculture workers, teachers, and micro-enterprise owners. Findings documented that non-essential work shutdowns forced almost all workers to stay home unpaid, and after reopening, many faced lower salaries and fewer hours. The survey also reflected a significant increase in domestic abuse and violence against women and children.

ICTD

Pakistan's Bid to Raise More Revenue: Wealth Taxes and Digital Public Infrastructure

A.Z. Farooqui, F. Chatha, F. Santoro, F. Muhammad & W.A. Bajwa · ICTD · 2025

Research output examining how Pakistan can increase government revenue through progressive wealth taxes and digital public infrastructure implementation. Draws on the first comprehensive nationwide survey on Pakistani attitudes toward wealth taxation (7,500 adults polled in 2024). Finds that over 40% of respondents support a general wealth tax, with support increasing for specific proposals when thresholds are included.

ICTD · LoGRI

The Political Economy of Progressive Property Tax Reform: Evidence from Pakistan

A. Cheema, S.K. Mohmand, A.Q. Khan, M.C. Best, A. Abbas, A.Z. Farooqui & M.A. Amin · ICTD Project Report

Research report on a property tax reform study embedded within a legislatively mandated reform in Punjab, investigating citizen, politician, and party worker support for higher and more progressive property taxation. Punjab's current effective property tax rate (0.07%) is well below international benchmarks. The research tests whether citizen misunderstanding of tax incidence explains support for regressive policies.

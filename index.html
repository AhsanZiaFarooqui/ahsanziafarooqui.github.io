I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the University of Sussex and a Doctoral Fellow at the International Centre for Tax and Development (ICTD) and Local Government Revenue Initiative (LoGRI); and a Graduate Fellow at the Institute for Development and Economic Alternatives Pakistan (IDEAS).

My research interests lie in public finance and state capacity, with a focus on how front-line bureaucrats' discretion shapes policy outcomes in Pakistan. In recent years, I have explored issues in the bureaucracies of policing, national and subnational taxation, and school-teacher recruitment, understanding their interactions with citizens and experimenting with ways in which technology can reduce bureuacratic discretion and increase public perception of fairness in Government systems.

I work closely with government partners and researchers.

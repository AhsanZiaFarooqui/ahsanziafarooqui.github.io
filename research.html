PhD Thesis

Property Tax, Bureaucratic Discretion, and Effective Progressivity in Punjab

PhD thesis project · With Excise & Taxation Department, Government of Punjab

Theoretical and empirical work on how the shift from Annual Rental Value to Capital Value taxation changes revenue, compliance, and the distribution of tax burdens when inspectors retain discretion over assessments and enforcement. This research is embedded within a legislatively mandated property tax reform in Punjab, conducted in close collaboration with the provincial government.

ICTD · Tax Compliance

Tracking Tax Compliance: Evidence from Pakistan's Track-and-Trace System

With collaborators at LUMS & ICTD

Administrative and firm-level evidence on how digital monitoring and invoicing affect VAT compliance, production, and firm behaviour in Pakistan's excise and sales tax system. Studies the role of digital public infrastructure in improving tax administration.

ICTD · Property Tax

The Political Economy of Progressive Property Tax Reform: Evidence from Pakistan

A. Cheema, S. Khan Mohmand, A.Q. Khan, M.C. Best, A. Abbas, A.Z. Farooqui & M.A. Amin

Investigates support for higher and more progressive property taxation among citizens, local-level politicians, and party workers in Punjab. Tests whether citizen misunderstanding of tax incidence explains support for regressive policies. Punjab's current effective property tax rate is 0.07%, significantly below international comparators.

Education · Political Economy

Merit-Based Teacher Recruitment and Bureaucratic Rules

With provincial education departments

Research on the political economy and implementation of merit-based recruitment rules for public-school teachers, combining policy timelines, protests data, and administrative records to understand how bureaucratic rules interact with local political pressures.

EGAP · Policing

EGAP MetaKeta IV: Community Policing, Dispute Resolution, and Public Trust in Punjab

PIs: A. Cheema, A. Hasanain & J.N. Shapiro (Princeton) · Research team member

Part of a multi-country coordinated trial (EGAP MetaKeta IV). Tests the efficacy of citizen-centric problem-oriented policing versus local alternative dispute resolution forums. Police beats randomly assigned to three arms: community policing, alternative dispute resolution, and control.

Governance · Gender

Community Policing and Gender Attitudes in Segregated Settings

With A. Cheema, S. Khan & J.N. Shapiro

A randomized community policing intervention combining segregation and integration-based approaches—creating separate community forums for women citizens while having male and female officers work together on integrated teams. Studies effects on gender attitudes, reporting of gender-based violence, and women's engagement with police.

Supervision of Research Assistants

IDEAS Pakistan

At IDEAS, I oversee multiple large-scale Randomized Controlled Trials aimed at improving governance, voting behavior, policing, and tax morale in Pakistan. I supervise monitoring and evaluation activities with a group of junior researchers, working in collaboration with leading economists from Pakistan and the United States. This includes training in survey design, data collection protocols, and analysis of experimental and administrative data.

Impact Evaluation Consulting

International NGOs & UN Agencies

I lead the consulting wing at IDEAS, providing evidence-based impact evaluation services to various international NGOs including UN bodies working in Pakistan. I help organizations design rigorous studies and evaluate program impact through primary evidence collected via opinion-based surveys and experimental methods.

Guest Lectures & Training

Sharing practical research expertise

Tax Administration, Data, and Empirical Methods

Guest lectures and training sessions on property tax systems, administrative data analysis, causal inference methods, and field experiment design. Topics cover the practical challenges of working with government agencies and implementing research at scale in developing countries.

Field Experiment Design and Implementation

Training on the design and implementation of large-scale field experiments, including randomization strategies, power calculations, survey instrument design, and managing field teams. Drawing on experience from policing, taxation, and education projects across multiple districts in Punjab, Pakistan.

Community Policing Does Not Build Citizen Trust in Police or Reduce Crime in the Global South
Graeme Blair, Jeremy M. Weinstein, Fotini Christia, Eric Arias, Emile Badran, Robert A. Blair, Ali Cheema, Ahsan Farooqui, Thiemo Fetzer, Guy Grossman, Dotan Haim, Zulfiqar Hameed, et al.
Science, Vol. 374, Issue 6571 (November 2021)

Six preregistered field experiments across the Global South tested the effects of community policing on citizen trust in police, willingness to report crime, and actual crime levels. The interventions reached approximately 9 million people across 516 treated areas in Brazil, Colombia, Liberia, Pakistan, the Philippines, and Uganda. Community policing did not improve citizen-police trust or reduce crime, suggesting that standard community policing models require complementary institutional reforms.

Community Policing and Citizen Trust in Pakistan
Ali Cheema, Ahsan Zia Farooqui, Ali Hasanain, Jacob N. Shapiro & Zulfiqar Hameed
Chapter 10 in Crime, Insecurity, and Community Policing. Cambridge University Press, 2022

This chapter presents findings from a randomized community policing intervention conducted in Punjab, Pakistan. It analyzes how community forums, dispute resolution mechanisms, and police-citizen engagement affect trust in police and perceptions of safety. The study is part of the EGAP MetaKeta IV multi-country coordinated trial on community policing.

Working Within Norms to Change Gender Attitudes: Evidence from Community Policing in a Segregated Setting
Ali Cheema, Ahsan Zia Farooqui, Sarah Khan & Jacob N. Shapiro
SSRN Working Paper, August 2024

Studies a randomized community policing intervention in Pakistan that demonstrates the promise of combining segregation and integration-based approaches through creating separate spaces for women citizens while having male and female officers work together on integrated teams. The intervention increased women's attendance at community forums and raised distinctive concerns around gender-based violence.

Property Tax, Bureaucratic Discretion, and Effective Progressivity in Punjab
Ahsan Zia Farooqui
PhD thesis chapter (in progress)

Examines how the transition from Annual Rental Value to Capital Value taxation in Punjab changes revenue, compliance, and the distribution of tax burdens when inspectors retain discretion over assessments and enforcement. Combines administrative data with quasi-experimental methods.

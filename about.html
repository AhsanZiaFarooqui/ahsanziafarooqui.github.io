About

PhD candidate in Economics focused on taxation, state capacity, and evidence-based policy

Profile

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the University of Sussex and a Doctoral Fellow at the International Centre for Tax and Development (ICTD) and the Local Government Revenue Initiative (LoGRI). I am also a Graduate Fellow at the Institute for Development and Economic Alternatives (IDEAS), Pakistan.

My research sits at the intersection of public finance and state capacity, with a focus on how frontline bureaucrats' discretion shapes policy outcomes in Pakistan. Methodologically, I combine administrative and survey data with causal inference tools including field experiments and quasi-experimental designs, and I use institutional detail to link empirical estimates to policy design.

I have more than seven years of experience working with governments and non-governmental organizations on multi-scaled short- and long-term impact evaluations aimed at increasing trust and cooperation between the public and government, improving the efficiency of governmental operations, and community-based development.

I have led research projects on policing, national and subnational taxation (including property and excise systems), digital public infrastructure, and public-school teacher recruitment. A recurring theme is testing how technology can reduce discretionary distortions and improve citizens' perceptions of fairness in government services.
